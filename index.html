WOOD PELLET

Wood Pellets are biofuels that can be used as the source of energy, created by compressing wood materials such as: dry wood, sawdust and lignin. Heat is applied in this process which causes lignin—a natural polymer found in wood—to act as a glue to hold the compressed particles together. Typically wood pellet has a size of 6 or 8 mm in diameter, and up to 40 mm in length.

It is one of the most cost-effective source of energy; Compared to fossil oil or natural gas, wood pellet can save about half of the expenses on fuel. When used in heating boilers, as well as wood pellet stoves, it transform more than 90 % of the energy contained in the wood pellet into usable heat.
